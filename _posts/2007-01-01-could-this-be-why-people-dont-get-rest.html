I have a quick confession. I came up with this idea before I wrote the previous blog about why people don’t value REST. The “revelation” that I discuss here sort of muddled/nullified the point I was trying to make in my previous post, but I’m hoping the sequence strengthens the point I make here. Follow? Didn’t think so :)

Scalability Concerns Are Dumb

One of the concerns when building a web application is scalability. Here’s a little hint: REST scales to the size of the freakin internet. You can’t beat that from a business standpoint or a technical one. So don’t bother. Any other web architecture is just holding you back.

Don’t Use The Web, Be The Web

I consider myself to get REST, but tonight I had a major moment of RESTful enlightenment. The web, as we know, is just a bunch of resources that you can locate and manipulate. So let’s say I build an application using the architecture of the web, exposing resources for public consumption. Internally it is of course an application, but externally it’s just resources that people can locate and play with. If my application is just resources, and the web is just resources, then my application doesn’t use the web, it’s not part of the web, it doesn’t sit on top of underneath or in between the web - it is the web.

To the web, there is no application. Your app is just some pseudo-random crap that makes resources available to the world.

The web is freakin huge. The web is so huge that people can’t fathom scaling to that size, so they look other places for “scalable” architectures. Even tougher though is idea that adopting the web’s architecture makes you the web itself.

Say it with me. My application is the web.
